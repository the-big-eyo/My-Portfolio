projects

inventory optimisation dashboard for minimising stockout and excess inventory

Business Problem:

A mid-sized retailer struggled with frequent stockouts on high-demand SKUs and excess stock on slow-moving items. This caused lost sales, higher carrying costs, and unpredictable lead times.

Objective:

Identify inventory risks, prioritise SKUs requiring immediate restocking, and recommend optimal safety stock and reorder points to reduce stockouts and holding costs

Data Source:

Inventory transaction records including SKU, daily sales, on-hand quantity, lead time, and reorder history

Tools & Techniques:

  • Data modelling & calculations in Power BI
  • DAX measures for safety stock & reorder point logic
  • Time-series analysis for demand variability
  • Visual dashboard with KPIs and trend charts

Key Deliverables:

  • Dashboard showing stock coverage, demand variability, reorder triggers, and inventory value heatmaps
  • Table of high-risk SKUs (stockout risk vs. stock level)
  • Demand forecast bands

Business Impact (Quantitative):

  • Identified 15 SKUs at critical risk of stockout within 2 weeks
  • Recommended new reorder levels that could reduce stockouts by ~25%
  • Suggested reductions in excess inventory worth ~$15,000 in holding costs annually

Reverse Logistics Performance Analysis For Returned Solar Product Operations

Business Problem:

Returned items were taking too long to process, and management lacked visibility into turnaround times, cost drivers, and regional performance. This was delaying refurbishment cycles and increasing costs

Goal:

Analyse return workflows to pinpoint bottlenecks and prioritise process improvements.

Data Source:

Return records with timestamps (request → pickup → arrival → completion), cost per return, region, and item outcomes (repaired, scrapped, refurbished)

Tools & Techniques:

  • Power BI for interactive visualization
  • KPI design (lead time measures, cost per return, return trends)
  • Funnel and trend charts highlighting bottlenecks

Key Findings:

  • Delays occur mainly between Request and Pickup stages
  • Certain regions incur 20–30% higher processing costs
  • Monthly return trend shows consistent spikes, suggesting seasonal effects

Business Impact:

  • Prioritise pickup process changes to reduce lead time by up to 18%
  • Target cost reduction actions in expensive regions
  • Use repair/refurbish insights to improve asset lifecycle & cost savings

Transportation Cost Efficiency Analysis for Logistics Network (sql project)

Business Problem:

The transport operations team needed clarity on which routes and trucks were cost-efficient and what factors drove operational cost increases.

Objective:

Identify high-cost routes, evaluate truck performance, and quantify the cost impact of distance & shipment weight.

Data Source:

Relational dataset with tables: Routes (origin, destination, distance), Trucks (capacity, fuel efficiency), Shipments (weight, cost, delivery dates).

Tools & techniques:

  • SQL for data extraction & aggregation
  • Window functions for performance ranking
  • Metrics calculated at route & truck level

Key Actions:

  • Calculated cost/km and cost/ton metrics
  • Ranked routes & trucks by efficiency
  • Identified patterns between distance and transportation costs

Outcomes & insights:

  • Determined that Route A had 30% higher cost/km than the network average
  • Identified Truck X as most efficient, suggesting reuse for long runs
  • Highlighted overweight shipments as a major cost factor

Business Impact:

  • Potential to re-allocate truck schedules to reduce average cost/km by ~12%
  • Data-backed recommendations for adjusting rates or routes
  • Fuel & maintenance-linked cost insights to support pricing and operations decisions
